STEPS OF DONATION

Identification of a Potential Donor

A patient is declared brain dead or experiences circulatory death, meeting the criteria for organ donation.

Evaluation and Consent

Doctors assess organ viability and obtain consent from the donor’s family or prior registration.

Medical Maintenance of Donor

The donor's body is maintained on life support to ensure organ viability until retrieval.

Organ Recovery

Surgeons carefully retrieve organs in a controlled medical environment to ensure their viability.

Organ Transportation

The organs are rapidly transported to transplant centers using specialized preservation techniques.

Transplant Surgery

The recipient undergoes transplant surgery, followed by careful post-operative monitoring.

About Organ Donation

Saving Lives Through Generosity

Why Organ Donation is Important

Organ donation is a selfless act that has the power to save and transform lives. Thousands of people wait for transplants every year, but the shortage of donors means many don’t survive. Just one donor can save up to 8 lives and improve many more through tissue donation. By choosing to be a donor, you provide hope and a second chance to those in need.

❤️

Heart

Pumps blood and oxygen throughout the body. A heart transplant can save lives.

🫁

Lungs

Responsible for breathing. Lung transplants help people with severe lung diseases.

🩸

Kidneys

Filter waste from blood. Kidney transplants remove the need for dialysis.

🦠

Liver

Vital for digestion & detox. It can regenerate, making living donation possible.

🧠

Brain

Brain donations contribute to medical research and understanding of neurological diseases.

👁️

Eyes

Corneal transplants restore sight to the visually impaired.

🦴

Bone Marrow

Helps treat leukemia and blood disorders through transplantation.

🩻

Small Intestine

The small intestine absorbs nutrients from food. Intestinal transplants help patients with severe digestive disorders.
